Why people leave their work?

The numbers don’t lie. At over 150 million, there are more people at work in the world. than ever before. However, employees continue to quit for what they see as better opportunities.

In 2017 a staggering 41.4 million. workers voluntarily left their jobs. Turnover trends demonstrate an 8.3% increase over 2017 and 88% increase since 2010. If you allow this trend to continue, the turnover rate will hit 35% by 2023, placing companies in continuous and enormous risks

Employee Turnover

Employee turnover is the loss of talent in the workforce over time. This includes any employee departure, including resignations, layoffs, terminations, retirements, location transfers

Repercusions

If and when any of those people actually leave, the consequences aren’t trivial. And they and can hit all size of businesses hard:

  1. Financially: Estimate backfilling roles costs employers roughly 20% of that position’s salary in hiring, recruiting, and onboarding costs.
  2. Productivity: could harm the performance overall of the business.
  3. Time: Time to fill a position is 36 days
